What you'll cover
Term-by-term breakdown
Term 1
Ukufunda Izindaba & Amagama
  • Ukufunda izindaba ezilula zokuqonda
  • Amagama amaningi: izinhlobo zamagama
  • Izibizo (nouns): izigaba nezinhlobo
  • Izenzo (verbs): isikhathi samanje
  • Ukubhala: indaba emfishane
Term 2
Amakhono Olimi & Izincazelo
  • Ukufunda izibalo nezindatshana
  • Izincazelo zamagama: izifaniso neziphambene
  • Izandiso (adjectives): ukuchaza izinto
  • Ukubhala: incwadi emfishane
  • Ukulalelisisa: imibuzo nezimpendulo
Term 3
Izikhathi & Ukubhala Okukhethekile
  • Izikhathi: esidlule, samanje, esizayo
  • Izandiso zendawo nesikhathi
  • Ukhungo lwamagama (word families)
  • Ukubhala: umbiko noma incwadi
  • Inkulumo: ukukhuluma ngokuqinisekile
Term 4
Izinkondlo & Ukubuyekezwa
  • Ukufunda nokujabulela izinkondlo
  • Isigqi, ukuklama namagama ahlukene
  • Ukubhala: indaba yobuciko
  • Ukubuyekezwa: izakhiwo zolimi
  • Ulimi lwasekhaya: inhlonipho namasiko

Study smarter
Tips for IsiZulu FAL
👂

Listen to IsiZulu as often as possible. Radio, music and conversations in Zulu train your ear and help you internalise how the language sounds and flows — something word lists alone can't do.

📝

Learn noun classes systematically. IsiZulu has a noun class system that governs agreement across sentences. Understanding the classes (abantu, izinto, etc.) makes grammar patterns much clearer.

🗣️

Practise speaking, even imperfectly. Speaking Zulu — even with errors — builds confidence and fluency far faster than only studying written rules. Find a practice partner if you can.

🌍

Connect language to culture. IsiZulu is rich in idioms, proverbs and cultural concepts. Learning the cultural context makes the language more meaningful and memorable — and examiners reward cultural awareness.
