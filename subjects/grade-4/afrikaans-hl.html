Overview
What to expect in Grade 4

Afrikaans Home Language in Grade 4 is taught to learners for whom Afrikaans is their primary home language. The CAPS curriculum covers four integrated skills: listening and speaking, reading and viewing, writing and presenting, and language structures and conventions. By the end of Grade 4, learners are expected to read Afrikaans texts independently, write structured paragraphs, and demonstrate a solid understanding of Afrikaans grammar. The content and skills mirror those of English HL but are applied within the Afrikaans language.

CAPS curriculum
Term-by-term breakdown
Term 1
Narrative texts, nouns & sentences
  • Reading and comprehension of short narrative texts in Afrikaans
  • Common nouns (soortnaamwoorde), proper nouns and collective nouns
  • Action verbs — identifying and using in context
  • Descriptive adjectives — colour, size, shape
  • Types of sentences: statement, question, command, exclamation
  • Writing a short personal narrative (persoonlike vertelling)
  • Capital letters and punctuation — consolidation
  • Oral: listening activities and show-and-tell in Afrikaans
Term 2
Descriptive writing & pronouns
  • Descriptive and informational text types in Afrikaans
  • Personal pronouns: ek, jy, hy, sy, dit, ons, julle, hulle
  • Possessive pronouns: my, jou, sy, haar, ons, julle, hul
  • Punctuation: commas in lists, apostrophes for possession
  • Prefixes and suffixes and their effect on meaning
  • Writing a descriptive paragraph about a person or place
  • Oral: describe a picture using complete Afrikaans sentences
Term 3
Informational texts & tenses
  • Reading informational and transactional texts (notices, letters) in Afrikaans
  • Present tense — habitual and continuous
  • Past tense — simple past with regular and irregular verbs
  • Future tense — sal, gaan
  • Direct speech vs indirect speech — introduction
  • Synonyms and antonyms (sinonieme en antonieme) — vocabulary extension
  • Subject-verb agreement in the present tense
  • Writing a short report or informal letter in Afrikaans
Term 4
Persuasive texts & poetry
  • Persuasive texts — identifying persuasive language and techniques
  • Poetry (poësie): stanzas, rhyme, rhythm, repetition
  • Figurative language: simile (vergelyking), alliteration (alliterasie)
  • Comprehension revision — literal and inferential questions
  • Conjunctions: en, maar, omdat, so, alhoewel
  • Revision of all four tenses
  • Writing a persuasive paragraph — giving reasons and opinions
  • Oral: short prepared speech or poem recitation in Afrikaans

Tips for Grade 4
📖

Lees die vrae voor die gedeelte. In begripstoetse maak dit dit baie makliker om die antwoord vinnig te vind. Onderstreep sleutelwoorde in elke vraag voordat jy begin lees.

✏️

Gebruik die merktoedeling as riglyn. A question worth (2) needs two separate points. 'n Vraag wat (1) werd is, benodig slegs een duidelike antwoord. Skryf nooit 'n enkele woord vir 'n 2-merkervraag nie.

📝

Haal altyd aan uit die teks wanneer jy bewys gee. Skryf: 'Die teks sê …' om die eksaminator presies te wys waar jy jou bewys gevind het.

🔤

Leer jou woordsoorte deur jou eie voorbeelde te maak. Skryf jou eie sinne met naamwoorde, werkwoorde en byvoeglike naamwoorde uit jou alledaagse lewe.

⏰

Kontroleer jou tye nadat jy geskryf het. Een van die mees algemene foute in Graad 4-skryfwerk is om tussen verlede en teenwoordige tyd te wissel. Gaan terug en kontroleer elke werkwoord.

🌱

Bou woordeskat deur 'n woordeboek te hou. Elke keer as jy 'n woord lees wat jy nie ken nie, skryf dit neer met sy betekenis en gebruik dit in 'n sin.
