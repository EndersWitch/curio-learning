Overview
What to expect in Grade 12

Grade 12 Life Orientation is the final year and all assessment is school-based — there is no external exam. The CAPS curriculum focuses on completing the personal, social and career development journey of the FET Phase, with particular emphasis on life after matric, application processes and managing the transition to tertiary education, work or entrepreneurship. Every LO submission counts directly toward your final mark.

CAPS curriculum
Term-by-term breakdown
Term 1
Personal development: matric pressure and resilience
  • Managing matric stress: evidence-based strategies — exercise, sleep, social support, time management
  • Burnout vs stress: how to recognise burnout early and what to do
  • Revision strategies backed by cognitive science: retrieval practice, spaced repetition, interleaving
  • Sleep and exam performance: why 8 hours matters more than an extra hour of study
  • Nutrition and hydration for cognitive performance during exam season
  • Physical Education: personal fitness programme — self-designed, self-monitored
  • Fitness assessment: final Senior Phase baseline comparison — celebrate improvement
  • Goal setting: SMART goals for matric results, gap year and tertiary entry
Term 2
Citizenship and social responsibility
  • SA's democratic challenges: voter apathy, corruption, state capture — what young people can do
  • Environmental responsibility: climate justice, COP commitments, SA's renewable energy transition
  • Digital citizenship: cyber law in SA, POPIA, responsible social media use at 18+
  • Community project: design, implement and reflect — minimum 8 hours of meaningful contribution
  • Gender-based violence: personal commitment to prevention and allyship
  • Volunteerism and civic engagement: how to find and participate in SA community organisations
  • Physical Education: team sport — final season, leadership roles, sportsmanship
  • Reflect on growth as a citizen across the FET Phase
Term 3
Applications and career transition
  • University application: NSFAS application, CAO/institutional applications — deadlines and process
  • TVET and FET college application: programmes, requirements, NCV and N-course pathways
  • Bursary application: write and submit at least one bursary application — reviewed and refined
  • Gap year planning: structured vs unstructured — how to make a gap year productive
  • Learnership and apprenticeship: how to find them, requirements, NQF levels
  • Interview skills: prepare for a tertiary or job interview — mock interview practice
  • Physical Education: individual activity of choice — maintain active lifestyle
  • Personal development plan: final version — subject results, career goals, action steps, timeline
Term 4
Transition, exam support and celebration
  • NSC exam support: time management strategies, dealing with exam anxiety, the night before
  • Post-exam planning: receiving results, understanding your APS, supplementary exams
  • Gap year vs immediate study: make a conscious, researched decision
  • Financial literacy for young adults: opening a bank account, budgeting independently, avoiding debt
  • Rights as an adult in SA: at 18 you can vote, sign contracts, access financial services
  • Physical Education: fun final activity — celebrate the physical journey
  • SBA portfolio: submit final portfolio — check all tasks are included and complete
  • Celebrate: you have completed 12 years of school 🌱

Tips for Grade 12
💪

Every SBA submission is your exam. There is no NSC paper. Every task is a direct mark. Submit everything, on time, to your best ability.

📝

Personal development plan must be specific. Vague plans score low. Subject results → APS → programme requirement → institution → application date → action taken. Be specific.

🌱

The community project needs a reflection. Implementation alone is not enough. Reflect on what you contributed, what you learned and how you grew. That reflection earns marks.

🎓

Start applications in Term 3, not Term 4. NSFAS, CAO and most bursaries close before matric results are released. Don't wait for results to apply.
